We weren't sure if we would like Panama. Nothing against the country, but the general trend during our travels throughout Central America was the need to <a href="/el%20salvador/roughweek/">escape the tropical heat</a> by <a href="/honduras/calmweek/">fleeing to the mountains</a>. A quick check of the map told us this wouldn't be much of an option. And so, before even crossing into the country, we limited our time in Panama to less than three weeks by agreeing to a shipping date to send our home to South America. Surely that would be more than enough time to explore the country without getting too burnt out in the heat.

Unfortunately for us, Panama became one of our favorite countries yet! Or, at least, western Panama did; we hardly got to the rest of the isthmus because we enjoyed our time in the Chiriquí region so much. To be fair to our initial concerns, the rest of Panama is quite hot (not so ideal for living in a dark car), but the regions around Volcán Barú were among our favorites in all of Central America. This meant that we did not get to any of the islands, beaches, or lakes that the country is famous for. And we skipped the entire region of the country east of the capital; one famous for its lakes, natural beauty, and indigenous craftworks. As always, there are so many amazing place to explore that -- even with all our travel time -- we hardly scratch the surface.
