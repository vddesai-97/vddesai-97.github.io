The countries of Central America have a long shared history that often doesn't neatly overlap with modern borders, from common Mayan ancestry to being consolidated by the Spanish into a single territory to post-colonial government rule that generally served the interests of the (usually American) wealthy class. With this shared history and similar cultures, four of the countries - Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, and Nicaragua - created the C-4, similar to the European Schengen Zone, allowing citizens and tourists alike an easier time traversing the area (although our experience has been more difficult here, but this may be becasue of the dog and the car). However, this comes with a caveat: we only had 90 days to cross these four large and interesting countries! After spending a longer time in Guatemala and El Salvador and deciding to take a week (and some travel days) to scuba dive in Honduras \comment{add link}, we had less than two weeks to spend in Nicaragua, a country we were both looking forward to!

We started in León, one of the old colonial cities from Spanish times. Not much to say here, as we largely rested after a long drive the day before from Tegucigalpa - the capital at the center of Honduras - all the way to León, with a 2-1/2 hour border stop for fun (again, not the easy with which one generally travels Europe). Plus the city was very hot, which is not fun in a black car and meant we did not get to go volcano boarding - sledding on volcano scree - because we could not leave Arya alone like that.
