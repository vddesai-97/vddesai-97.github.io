Ahh a week to be had. A sister to know and love. Who knows. \comment{lol, still workshopping?}

Taco Bell. Our first stop after picking up my sister, Tulsi, at the airport. Obviously. With a 4 hour drive ahead of us after an already long day of flying for Tulsi, we were going to need sustenance. 

Our first (actual) destination: Monteverde. Known for its cloud forests, diverse array of birds, and wildlife. Unfortunately, wildlife and bird watching were not in our luck this time around. We spent hours meandering around forests shrouded in thick fog, listening to bird sounds at Curi Cancha Reserve, hiking steep steep (1100 ft elevation gain -- too steep for us Desais) trails for waterfall views, and occasionally spotted a 'darting Tulsi' when one heard big large black beetles flying toward us. We did spot a sloth, a keel-billed toucan, and a couple of cool looking by the time we left Monteverde. \comment{you're gonna have to figure out how to reconcile this sentence with the "not in our luck" sentence, cause most people would think this is awesome}

On the road for another long drive, we stopped at Nuevo Arenal (link) where Clark and I had spent a bit of time at before leaving to pick Tulsi up. There we stopped for pizzas and beer with a lakeview before heading to the La Fortuna area. Due to a very busy day at the restaurant, we ended up spending an hour longer than we had anticipated, after which the staff graciously offered us brownies as an appreciation for our patience and which we very excitedly accepted (who can say no to brownies!?). This meant though that we had reached our town of Colonial Trinidad in the dark...

We try not to drive in the dark, especially in unknown areas and on unknown roads. Largely this comes down to reduced visibility, especially as roads throughout Central America can be inconsistent \link{topes poem}. Well, this night tells you why this is such an important rule. We were a little lost due to some poor cell signal and crummy google map directions, and, as we were trying to back out of a road to do a u-turn, we rolled into a deep ditch. As in, the tire hardly touching the ground deep. Oh dear. Unable to get the car out , we would need to call a tow truck or something which we did not want to deal with as we were all extremely exhausted and just wanted to get to our beds. Thank god for the kindness of people. As I was standing outside, evaluating what could be done and slowly losing hope as the car was so tilted and the tires looked to be going in opposite directions, neighboring folks started coming out as they had heard us hit the ditch. Motorcyclists driving by stopped and came over to see what was going on and how to help. Within minutes, roughly a dozen men (giving off Big Dad Energy) \comment{funny?} and I were appraising the situation of the car and trying to push the car out as C revved the engine. Eventually, an older man who was passing by in a truck had an idea and the tools to get our car out. Unfortunately, the first iteration involved our rear bumper, (the same one damaged in Mexico \comment{link}), but we pivoted, wrapping the tow rope around a cement pillar and attaching to our front bumper to torque the Stinkbug out as he revved his truck and many of the other men pushed. And wah, our car was out of the ditch. My Spanish was nonexistent (as tends to happen when my brain is too exhausted) so the resulting conversation was stilted and difficult, but expressing gratitude and joy easily transcends language barriers.

The next morning, we stayed in - a decsion easily reached - and watched Zootopia 2, which was a great movie to prime us for our safari boat tour that evening. We saw so much wildlife along the river; many species of birds, two different types of monkeys, a crocodile, a close-up sloth, and even a basilisk! The next day we did a chocolate tour, where we had cacoa tea, hot chocolate, freshly pressed sugarcane juice, and made our own chocolate truffles, which was very fun and very delicious. Being the rainy season, the our evening plans got rained out, but we made the best of it indoors with more pizza and a couple games of poker. The final day was upon us and we spent it hiking Arenal Volcano where we saw lava fields, a pair of Giant Curassows, and some cute poison-dart frogs followed by shopping at artesenal markets in La Fortuna.
